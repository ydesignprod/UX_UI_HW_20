Redline annotation

  • Hypothesis

    Veterans with accessibility challenges need a clear application process. Creating an account where they could return to their application and save changes could simplify and streamline the process. Knowing what documents are needed to apply on the first page will allow them to plan for the process and may reduce the number of abandoned, unfinished applications.

  • Problem Statement

    We believe that by creating an easy application process with clearly defined steps, required documents identified early on, and a login portal veterans with accessibility challenges will achieve greater application success and reduce the number of abandoned applications.

Shelter to Soldier is a 501C3 non profit organization. Our mission is to "Save Lives Two at a Time" We rescue dogs from local shelters and train them to be psychiatric service dogs for veterans with PTSD, TBI or psychological injuries associated with traumatic service experiences.


Can you give us a short overview of your program?

We have a few types of "users" . One would be people looking to support us via donations or making purchases of our merchandise. The other would be Veterans looking for help and wanting to apply to be a part of the program.


Can you tell us a bit about the demographics of your users?

We receive a lot of donations through our website, either one time donations or monthly. We do get a lot of requests to make the donation in honor or memory of a loved one or beloved pet. Our software doesn't allow for the tribute information to be included, so the donors have to email us separately in order to fulfill this wish.
